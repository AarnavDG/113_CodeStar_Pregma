First Trimester

The first trimester (weeks 1-12) is a time of rapid development for your baby. You may experience symptoms like nausea, fatigue, and breast tenderness.

Second Trimester

The second trimester (weeks 13-26) is often called the "golden period" of pregnancy. You may feel more energetic and start to notice your baby's movements.

Third Trimester

The third trimester (weeks 27-40) is the final stretch. Your baby grows rapidly, and you may experience discomfort as your body prepares for labor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about pregnancy, fitness, and nutrition.

1. What are the early signs of pregnancy? ▼

Early signs of pregnancy include missed periods, nausea, fatigue, breast tenderness, and frequent urination. If you suspect you're pregnant, take a pregnancy test or consult your doctor.

2. What foods should I avoid during pregnancy? ▼

During pregnancy, avoid raw or undercooked meat, unpasteurized dairy, certain fish high in mercury (like swordfish), and excessive caffeine. Always consult your doctor for personalized advice.

3. Can I exercise during pregnancy? ▼

Yes, moderate exercise is generally safe and beneficial during pregnancy. Activities like walking, swimming, and prenatal yoga are great options. Always consult your doctor before starting any exercise routine.

4. How much weight should I gain during pregnancy? ▼

Weight gain during pregnancy depends on your pre-pregnancy weight. On average, women with a healthy BMI should gain 25-35 pounds. Your doctor can provide personalized recommendations.

5. What should I pack in my hospital bag? ▼

Pack essentials like comfortable clothing, toiletries, snacks, important documents, and items for your baby (e.g., clothes, diapers). Check out our hospital bag checklist for a detailed guide.
